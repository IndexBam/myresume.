I spent most of my teen years deciding what I actually liked. A lot of questions were raised, because I just had too many passions.

Since young age, I have always loved mathematics and had good results in exams and even some awards in the area. For most people I was clearly going to be an engineer.

When I was 12, I started learning English. I didn't know at the time but it was only the start of my love journey with learning languages. For a bunch of people, I would be a teacher.

Still when I was 12, I also loved psicology. Understanding human beings and trying to help them was something I would love to do. My parents were worried I wouldn't make a living as a therapist.

But everything changed when I was in high school. I just fell in love with biology and decided I wanted to apply it to studying the human body and helping people with it. Ever since this day, I shut all the voices screaming what I should be and pointed out what deep down I knew I really wanted. I was deciding, for the first time. I wanted to be I doctor.

Things get complicated when you decide. It is scary. And, although I knew what I wanted, I had a feeling it was not everything I was. I stated to question my career decision, when my love for mathematics raised when I started studying python as a 16 year old teenager. I just loved it at first sight and started to thing I should switch to engineering. I was happy with being engineer. I did always love mathematics and now programming. It was the perfect decision. If only my other love had not started to kick in. I really liked it all, but med school was SO appealing too. I was confuse and scared.

Things felt clearer when I saw that I would be frustated if I did not become a doctor. I had the biggest chance now that I was studying for the Brazilian version of the SAT and I decided to hold on to it and become the doctor I had for a long time dreamt of being. It took me three years of great effort and renouncement, but now I've got into med school and I could not feel better about it and about my decision

In spite of my accomplishment, I had almost a gap year before getting into med school, and during this time I have nurtured my love for programming. Rediscovering this love of mine, has been a great experience and everyday I am happy to study programming, develope algorithms and websites. I am just so happy to create something and see it working. I have the goose bumps every time I do it. So I can affirm: I really am the two things; programmer and a med student. And I really love being both.
